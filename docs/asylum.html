Project Asylum

About

Project Asylum is a first-person psychological horror game where the asylum itself is an active antagonist. Explore the dynamically changing hallways of this abandoned mental institution, uncover secrets with only a UV flashlight, and escape the nightmare alive.

Project Details

  • Role: Gameplay Programmer (Movement, Sound Implementation, Gameplay Mechanics)
  • Team Size: 15
  • Engine: Unreal Engine 5 (Blueprints)
  • Development Time: 7 weeks

Project Asylum

Introduction

In Project Asylum, you play as an employee sent to verify the demolition of an abandoned mental institution. But things quickly spiral into chaos as the building itself shifts, traps, and terrifies you. Equipped with only a UV flashlight, players must navigate eerie hallways, solve environmental puzzles, and uncover the institution's dark secrets while avoiding unseen dangers lurking in the shadows.

Gameplay and Features

  • Dynamic Environment: The asylum's layout changes in real-time, disorienting players and enhancing tension.
  • Immersive Sound Design: Sound cues play a vital role, from footsteps to slamming doors and eerie background noises.
  • Interactive Mechanics: "Players interact with objects like flashlights, doors, and environmental prompts, utilizing a refined interaction system."
  • Psychological Horror: The game focuses on atmosphere and psychological tension rather than jump scares.

Development Process, My Contributions & Lessons Learned

As a gameplay programmer for Project Asylum, I was responsible for implementing key systems that enhanced immersion and gameplay. Besides all the smaller ones, here's a breakdown of my biggest contributions:

  • Movement Mechanics: Developed smooth movement functions, including sprinting, crouching, and backwards movement. Integrated camera effects to heighten immersion during sprinting or crouching.
  • Sound Implementation: Implemented a comprehensive sound system including footsteps, flashlight sounds, door slams, and environmental cues to increase tension and atmosphere, and many more!
  • Environmental Horror Triggers & Dynamic Events: These are the blueprint-driven events that dynamically react to the player's presence, reinforcing the atmosphere and unpredictability of the game.
  • Prompt System Adjustments: Enhanced the interaction system, allowing players to engage with objects like flashlights, doors, and notes, providing dynamic gameplay feedback.
  • End Game Screen: Created the end-game UI screen using Blueprints to trigger after completing or failing the game.

This project sharpened my skills in Blueprint scripting, event-driven design, and integrating gameplay mechanics with immersive sound design. It also provided valuable experience collaborating with level designers and artists to deliver a cohesive horror experience.
